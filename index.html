Safe-Speed Choreography Assistant

Arduino-based wearable system for dance safety

Overview

This project combines my passion for dance and biomedical engineering by creating a wearable device that transforms how dancers train safely. This Arduino-based smart device uses sophisticated motion sensors to track movement patterns and speed, providing instant feedback to prevent joint and muscle injuries. Already proven effective with young dancers, it represents the future of safe dance training.

How It Works

1. Smart Sensing

  • Advanced motion sensors monitor your movements in real-time
  • Tracks both angular velocity (rotation) and linear acceleration
  • Built-in gyroscope and accelerometer provide precise 3D movement data

2. Intelligent Processing

  • Arduino-based microcontroller analyzes movement patterns
  • Custom algorithms continuously evaluate motion parameters
  • Compares current movement against pre-set safety thresholds

3. Instant Feedback

  • Provides immediate auditory alerts when safety limits are exceeded
  • Warns dancers about:
    • Excessive joint angles
    • Dangerous movement speeds
    • Rapid directional changes

4. Safe Practice

  • Dancers adjust their movements based on real-time feedback
  • Helps maintain proper form and tempo
  • Builds muscle memory for safer dance techniques

5. Easy Usage

  • Lightweight, wearable design
  • Attaches securely to arms or legs
  • Powered by long-lasting LiPo battery
  • Configurable thresholds for different skill levels

Hardware Architecture

The system's hardware architecture consists of the following key components interconnected to enable precise motion tracking and real-time feedback:

Key Components:

  • Arduino Nano 33 BLE Sense Rev2 microcontroller with built-in Gyroscope and Accelerometer
  • MT3608 DC-DC step up boost power converter module
  • TP4056 battery charger module
  • LiPo 3.7V rechargeable battery
  • Piezo electronic buzzer

Operation

The device uses an Arduino Nano 33 BLE Sense Rev2 microcontroller to capture real-time motion data from its built-in inertial measurement unit (IMU). The Arduino continuously measures the dancer's arm angle and triggers a piezo electronic buzzer to beep whenever the movement goes outside a preset range, providing immediate audio feedback during choreography practice. The system is powered by a 3.7 V LiPo rechargeable battery, which connects to a TP4056 charging module for safe recharging via USB. The MT3608 DC-DC step-up converter boosts the battery voltage to a stable 5 V to power the Arduino and buzzer. Together, these components create a compact, battery-powered wearable that helps dancers maintain correct posture and rhythm through both real-time feedback and recorded motion analysis.

Iterations & Learnings

  1. Consolidated sensors to reduce bulk. I began with a separate MPU6050 gyroscope and accelerometer, but the extra wiring increased the device's size. Switching to an Arduino Nano 33 BLE Sense (which integrates both sensors) streamlined the design and reduced the footprint by about 20%.
  2. Optimized power for wearability. A 5000mAh power bank was too heavy for a wearable prototype, so I moved to a compact 3.7V 1500mAh LiPo battery charged via a TP4056. Because the Arduino required 5V, I added an MT3608 DC-DC boost converter — a practical lesson in voltage regulation for embedded systems.
  3. Improved mechanical stability through iteration. Velcro straps initially failed during movement. After testing, I learned to stack the modules and secure them with rubber bands, which significantly improved stability and reduced the device's footprint.
  4. Enhanced user appeal with lightweight enclosure. The early prototype looked unfinished, which could reduce engagement for younger users. To increase comfort and appeal, I enclosed the electronics in a lightweight Mickey Mouse rubber case, making the device more inviting to wear.

These iterative changes reflect a user-centered design approach: optimizing sensor integration, power, mechanical reliability, and aesthetics to make the prototype practical for dance rehearsal.
